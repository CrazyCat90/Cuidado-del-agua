Glossary: The Importance of Water

Water Conservation: The practice of using water efficiently to reduce waste and ensure sustainable use for future generations.

Hydration: The process of providing adequate water to the body to maintain proper bodily functions and prevent dehydration.

Water Scarcity: The condition where the demand for water exceeds the available supply, leading to limited access to clean and safe water sources.

Sustainable Water Management: The approach to managing water resources in a way that meets current needs without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.

Water Pollution: The contamination of water bodies, such as rivers, lakes, and oceans, by harmful substances, chemicals, or pathogens, resulting in adverse effects on aquatic ecosystems and human health.

Access to Clean Water: The provision of safe and reliable water sources for drinking, sanitation, and hygiene, essential for human health, well-being, and economic development.

Ecosystem Services: The benefits provided by natural ecosystems, including water purification, flood control, and habitat for biodiversity, which are essential for supporting life on Earth.

Waterborne Diseases: Illnesses caused by pathogenic microorganisms that are transmitted through contaminated water, such as cholera, typhoid fever, and dysentery.

Desalination: The process of removing salt and other impurities from seawater or brackish water to produce freshwater suitable for human consumption and irrigation.

Water-Energy Nexus: The interconnected relationship between water and energy systems, highlighting the dependence of each sector on the other and the need for integrated management approaches to ensure sustainability.
